Nigerian Jollof Recipe

Nigerian Jollof Rice is a legendary one-pot dish that is popular across West Africa, known for its rich flavors and vibrant colors. This one-pot meal is made with rice, tomatoes, and a blend of spices, often served with fried plantains, salad, or grilled meats. Here's a simple recipe to make your own delicious Nigerian Jollof at home.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (400g) diced tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup tomato paste
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1-2 cups mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, bell peppers)
  • Optional: cooked chicken or shrimp for protein

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Set aside.
  2. In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and saut√© until translucent.
  3. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  4. Add the diced tomatoes and tomato paste to the pot. Cook for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the mixture thickens.
  5. Add the chicken broth, thyme, curry powder, bay leaf, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine.
  6. Add the rinsed rice to the pot and stir well to coat the rice with the tomato mixture.
  7. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let it simmer for about 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and has absorbed all the liquid.
  8. In the last 5 minutes of cooking, add the mixed vegetables (and cooked protein if using) to the pot. Stir gently to combine.
  9. Once everything is cooked, remove from heat and let it sit covered for an additional 5 minutes.
  10. Fluff the rice with a fork before serving. Enjoy your Nigerian Jollof!
